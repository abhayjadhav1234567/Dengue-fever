Fight Dengue Together

Dengue fever is a mosquito-borne disease that affects millions of people worldwide every year. Learn how to protect yourself and your community.

Dengue Prevention

Protect yourself and your family with these effective prevention strategies

Eliminate Breeding Sites

Mosquitoes that spread dengue breed in standing water. Regularly check and remove stagnant water from:

  • Flower pots and plant saucers
  • Water storage containers
  • Discarded tires and bottles
  • Clogged roof gutters

Home Protection

Make your home a dengue-free zone with these measures:

  • Install window and door screens
  • Use mosquito nets while sleeping
  • Use household insecticides
  • Use air conditioning when possible

Personal Protection

Reduce your risk of mosquito bites with these strategies:

  • Wear long-sleeved shirts and pants
  • Use EPA-registered repellents
  • Avoid outdoor activities at dawn and dusk
  • Use mosquito coils and vaporizers

Dengue Symptoms

Recognizing symptoms early can lead to prompt medical attention and better outcomes

Mild Symptoms

These usually appear 4-10 days after being bitten by an infected mosquito:

  • High fever (104°F/40°C)
  • Severe headache
  • Pain behind the eyes
  • Muscle and joint pain
  • Nausea and vomiting

Severe Symptoms

Severe dengue is a medical emergency. Watch for these warning signs:

  • Severe abdominal pain
  • Persistent vomiting
  • Bleeding from gums or nose
  • Blood in vomit or stool
  • Difficulty breathing

Treatment & Care

If you suspect dengue, follow these guidelines:

  • Seek medical advice immediately
  • Rest and stay hydrated
  • Use acetaminophen for pain relief
  • Avoid aspirin and ibuprofen
  • Follow your doctor's instructions
